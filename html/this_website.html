This Website

Why take inspiration from the UK Government for website design?

The Government Design Service (GDS) - along with all public-sector design teams - are uniquely positioned in the world of interaction design:

  • They are unlikely to abuse 'Dark Patterns' (you can read about deceptive design on mozilla.org) since their organisation is not oriented around profit.*
  • Usability and accessibility for end users are some of their employer's Key Performance Indicators (KPIs) instead of an afterthought.
  • Due to the many faceted functions of government, their work is interdisciplinary and therefore likely to be applicable in various contexts.
  • They have a unique opportunity to spend time conducting design research, where the private sector might prefer to rush an artefact to production.

*You could argue that HMRC is 'profit-seeking'.

Okay, but why for your personal site?

Imitation is the highest form of flattery, I suppose...

Getting a website to be usable is a high priority for me; taking inspiration from the GDS' design system gives this website a solid foundation to build upon.

From an interface design perspective, it is also a great opportunity to explore the design thinking and implementation details behind usable website components.
