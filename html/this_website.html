This Website

Why take inspiration from the UK Government for website design?

The Government Digital Service (GDS) - along with all public-sector digital teams - are uniquely positioned in the world of interaction design:

  • They are unlikely to abuse 'Dark Patterns' (you can read about deceptive design on mozilla.org) since their organisation is not oriented around profit.*
  • Usability and accessibility for end users are their Key Performance Indicators (KPIs), rather than an afterthought.
  • Due to the many faceted functions of government, their work is multidisciplinary and therefore likely to have applicability in various contexts.
  • They have a unique opportunity to spend time conducting design research, where the private sector may instead rush an artefact to production.

*One could argue that HMRC is 'profit-seeking'.

Why for your personal site?

Imitation is the highest form of flattery.

Getting a website to be usable is a high priority for me; taking inspiration from the GDS' design system gives this website a solid research-backed foundation to build upon.

From an design perspective, it is an opportunity to explore the design thinking and implementation details of highly usable website components.
